## CRITICAL terminology fix — 2026-05-15 21:21 CDT

The mod is called "parry-tell-probe" and named after PARRY mechanics, which
in Elden Ring are bound to **L2 (weapon art)**, NOT L1 (block).

- **L1 = block.** Raises shield. Mitigates damage but does NOT stagger
  the enemy. Will always "graze off with chip damage" against ANY attack,
  parryable or not.
- **L2 with a parry-capable weapon art** (Buckler Parry, Carian
  Retaliation, Storm Wall, etc.) = parry. Catches incoming attack
  during its active frames, staggers enemy, opens riposte (R1) window.

The 33-67ms windows in the parry DB are the frames during which the
boss's attack is **catchable by L2 parry**, not "can be blocked by L1."
Every previous Claude conversation that said "press L1 to the cue" was
WRONG — should have been L2.

This is a Claude-level documentation bug. The DB itself is correct
(it's extracted from the game's TAE data, which the game uses for its
actual parry collision detection). The mod is correct. The chat prose
about how to use it was wrong.

**Carry-forward rule:** all future Claude sessions on this project must
say "L2" when discussing how the player responds to the cue. Never L1.

## Open question for next session

**Does anim 4003103 actually have a parryable window?** Three hypotheses:

a) **DB is right, Josh was blocking (L1) instead of parrying (L2) on
   both 4003103 cues.** Expected outcome: glance with chip damage, no
   stagger. Matches what Josh observed.
b) **DB has a variant mismatch.** raw `anim_id = 4003103` corresponds
   to multiple in-game animation slots; DB's `a004_003103` window only
   applies to one of them. Other variants are non-parryable.
c) **DB has a false positive at 4003103.** Window in TAE data exists
   but the game's collision system doesn't actually honor it.

Hypothesis (a) is the most likely a priori and easiest to test.
